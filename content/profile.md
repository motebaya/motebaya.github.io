Hey hey, I'm **Motebaya**, or more commonly known as **davins**. I've been into programming since August 2019. Let me tell you a bit..

Back then, I started as a [script kiddie](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Script_kiddie) grabbing someone else's source code, tweaking it however I pleased, and proudly sharing it with friends.

There was a moment when I wanted to modify someone's [Python](https://www.python.org/) code, but I couldn't read the file because it was compiled into **object code** or more precisely **bytecode/pyc**. I got curious, and suddenly a bunch of questions popped into my head, 'How can I open this file? How does it work? How can code that's completely unreadable by a normal human brain run without any issues?'.

Since then, I got serious and consistent about learning Python, aiming to dive into [RE (reverse engineering)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reverse_engineering). By mid-2020, I had a decent understanding, but that wasn't enough. I shifted my interest to another programming language, PHP. I learned [CLI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Command-line_interface) and **web servers** simultaneously until the end of 2020.

From late 2019 to the end of 2020, I did all of this on my Android device, an 8.1 (Oreo) 32-bit with 2/16 GB RAM, which I bought with my first salary. The best android terminal I knew and used at that time is [Termux](https://termux.dev/en/). I also used editors like [Nano](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nano) and [Vim](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vim_(text_editor)), along with some Android code editor apps, despite their limitations. But that wasn't enough to make me give up. I joined several online communities and even created my own. Talking with people who have knowledge is really enjoyable.

As early 2021 rolled around, my real-life responsibilities increased, leaving me with insufficient rest. I disbanded the community I created and left all the others.

A year passed until early 2022, I already had a powerful desktop, and my busy schedule began to ease. I revisited everything I had learned, although I couldn't stay as consistent and competent in programming as before.

Up until now, I've got a good grasp of languages like **JavaScript, PHP, Ruby, and Python**. I'm also familiar with **web scraping, bot automation, web servers, CLI, RE, scripting (bash)** and more. I only learn what I feel necessary and truly needed. Sometimes I take on part-time/freelance work, create private projects to sell, and work on public personal projects just for fun during my free time.

And during holidays, you can call me a **weekend coder**. I'm not a genius and I don't have a higher education or a degree in IT because my real life job has nothing to do with it.
